Christmas is a very important event in the Philippines, and people in Manila celebrate it with great enthusiasm and festivity. Here are some of the things that people in Manila do during the Christmas season:

1. Attend Simbang Gabi - This is a nine-day novena of Masses that starts on December 16 and ends on Christmas Eve. Many people attend the early morning Masses, which start as early as 4:00 a.m.
2. Decorate Homes and Streets - People in Manila decorate their homes with Christmas lights, lanterns, and other decorations. Many streets are also adorned with Christmas lights and decorations, creating a festive atmosphere.
3. Attend Christmas Parties - Filipinos love to celebrate with their family and friends during the Christmas season. Many people attend Christmas parties and gatherings, where they enjoy food, drinks, and games.
4. Give Gifts - Gift-giving is a big part of Christmas in the Philippines. Many people exchange gifts with their family and friends, and it is also common for employers to give their employees a Christmas bonus or gift.
5. Prepare Special Food - Filipino cuisine has many special dishes that are prepared during the Christmas season, such as lechon (roasted pig), bibingka (rice cake), and puto bumbong (purple rice cake).
6. Watch Fireworks - On Christmas Eve, many people in Manila watch fireworks displays to welcome the arrival of Christmas Day.

Overall, Christmas is a time for celebration and togetherness in Manila. It is a time for people to come together, share their blessings, and spread joy and happiness to those around them.
