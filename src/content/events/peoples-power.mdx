### A Historic Day Enshrined in Time

The People’s Power Anniversary holds great significance in the annals of Philippine history. This momentous day commemorates the peaceful revolution of 1986, a turning point that toppled the dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos and ushered in a new era of democracy in the country. Celebrated annually on February 25, it serves as a poignant reminder of the courage and sacrifice of the Filipino people in their pursuit of freedom.

### Unveiling the Spirit: Commemoration and Reflection

The People’s Power Anniversary commands reverence as a national public holiday, with diverse events and activities echoing throughout the nation. A focal point of the commemoration is the solemn ceremony held at the iconic People Power Monument in Quezon City—an enduring symbol of gratitude to the heroes of the revolution. Visitors from far and wide gather to pay their respects on this hallowed day.

Beyond the monument, vibrant parades, soul-stirring concerts, and captivating cultural shows animate the streets. Educational institutions also play a vital role, organizing special programs to enlighten students about the profound impact of the People’s Power Revolution on the course of Philippine history.
