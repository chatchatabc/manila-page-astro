Fête de la Musique de Manila is an annual music festival held in the Philippines that celebrates the universal language of music. The festival was first held in Manila in 1994 and has since become one of the most anticipated cultural events in the country.

The festival features a diverse range of musical genres and showcases both established and up-and-coming local and international artists. From jazz to rock, hip-hop to classical, there is something for everyone at Fête de la Musique de Manila.

Fête de la Musique de Manila is part of the international Fête de la Musique network, which began in France in 1982 and has since spread to more than 700 cities in over 120 countries. The festival's aim is to promote music as a means of cultural exchange and unity, and to provide a platform for artists to showcase their talent.

The festival is usually held in late June and takes place in various venues and outdoor stages across Manila. The festival's main stage is often located at the historic Intramuros, where musicians from different genres and backgrounds come together to perform for thousands of music lovers from all over the country.

What makes Fête de la Musique de Manila unique is its open, free and inclusive approach. The festival is open to everyone, regardless of age, gender, or social status. It is a celebration of music and the diversity of cultures in the Philippines.

Fête de la Musique de Manila is not only a showcase of talent but also an opportunity for music enthusiasts to discover new sounds and engage with the vibrant music scene in the country. The festival provides a platform for local artists to gain exposure, while also introducing international musicians to the Philippine music scene.

Overall, Fête de la Musique de Manila is a must-see event for music lovers and a testament to the vibrant and diverse cultural scene in the Philippines. If you're planning to visit Manila in late June, be sure to add this festival to your itinerary!
