The National Museum of Natural History is located in Manila, Philippines and is part of the National Museum of the Philippines. It opened its doors to the public in May 2018 and has since become a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The museum is housed in the former Department of Tourism building and features 12 exhibition halls that showcase the Philippines' rich biodiversity.

One of the highlights of the museum is the Tree of Life exhibit, a towering 70-foot DNA-inspired sculpture that greets visitors upon entry. The exhibit features an interactive touch screen display that allows visitors to explore the evolutionary history of the Philippines' flora and fauna.

The museum's collection features over 10,000 specimens, including a variety of mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, and insects. Some of the notable specimens on display include a rare megamouth shark, a skeleton of a dugong, and the remains of the largest crocodile ever recorded in the country.

Aside from the permanent exhibits, the museum also hosts temporary exhibitions throughout the year. Recent exhibits include "Wildlife Photographer of the Year," which showcases stunning photographs of animals from around the world, and "Huni sa Dilim," an interactive exhibit that explores the sounds of the Philippine rainforest.

Admission to the National Museum of Natural History is free for all visitors, making it accessible to everyone. The museum is open from Tuesdays to Sundays, from 10 am to 5 pm.

In conclusion, the National Museum of Natural History is a fascinating destination that showcases the Philippines' rich biodiversity. Whether you're a nature enthusiast or simply curious about the country's flora and fauna, the museum offers a unique and educational experience.
